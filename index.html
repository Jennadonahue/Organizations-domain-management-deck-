Why One Account Can’t Support Multiple Brands

Klaviyo’s account model is intentionally built around a single brand. Here’s what breaks when you force multiple brands into one.

Click any row to expand the full problem and solution

1
Consent and suppression breaks across brands One unsubscribe silences a customer across every brand — even ones they never opted out of.
Click to expand+
❌ The Problem

In a single account, Klaviyo’s suppression logic operates globally. If a customer unsubscribes from one brand’s emails, that suppression applies across every brand in the account — even brands the customer never interacted with. There is no way to scope consent to a specific brand inside one account. This creates compliance risk, destroys cross-brand marketing effectiveness, and cannot be fixed with features — it is an architectural limitation.

✅ How Organizations Solves It

Organizations preserves a dedicated account per brand, meaning suppression lists are fully isolated by design. An unsubscribe from Wayfair has zero impact on Birch Lane — they are separate accounts with separate consent records. The Consent Management Framework (Milestone 1 — Committed) introduces org-level infrastructure for storing and managing consent, enabling brand-specific opt-in forms and opt-out pages that respect each brand’s boundaries. Customers can be subscribed to Brand A and unsubscribed from Brand B simultaneously with no bleed — something that is architecturally impossible in a single account.

2
Sender reputation bleeds between brands One brand’s poor performance damages deliverability for every other brand — across both domain and IP reputation.
Click to expand+
❌ The Problem

Sender reputation is determined by two factors: domain reputation and IP reputation. Even if Klaviyo supported multiple sending domains inside one account — giving each brand its own domain — all brands would still share the same IP pool. A poorly performing campaign from Brand A generates spam complaints and low engagement that degrades the shared IP reputation, directly hurting deliverability for Brand B and Brand C regardless of their individual domain health. Fixing the domain problem alone doesn’t fix the IP problem. And IP pools are just one example — the same issue applies to every piece of sending infrastructure across every channel: SMS sender IDs, phone numbers, app integrations. Each layer of infrastructure compounds the problem.

✅ How Organizations Solves It

Because each brand has its own dedicated Klaviyo account, each brand gets fully isolated sending infrastructure — its own sending domain, its own IP pool, and its own channel configuration. Brand A’s deliverability problems cannot touch Brand B. This is why supporting multiple domains inside a single account would not actually solve the problem — it would only address domain reputation while leaving IP reputation, SMS infrastructure, and every other channel still shared and vulnerable. Organizations solves the whole problem, not just the visible surface of it.

3
Reporting and attribution becomes unreliable Revenue and engagement blend together — you can’t tell which brand is winning or losing.
Click to expand+
❌ The Problem

All analytics in Klaviyo are scoped to a single account. Revenue attribution, flow performance, campaign engagement, and profile metrics are all aggregated across every brand in the account with no clean way to separate them. A $500 order — was it driven by Brand A’s campaign or Brand B’s flow? There is no reliable answer. Leadership loses the ability to evaluate brand-level performance, identify underperforming brands, or make data-driven investment decisions across the portfolio. The data is irreparably blended at the point of collection.

✅ How Organizations Solves It

Cross Brand Reporting (Milestone 1 — Committed) introduces org-level analytics that sit on top of clean, brand-separated data. Because each brand has its own account, revenue attribution, engagement metrics, and profile data are collected cleanly at the brand level first. The org layer then aggregates those clean data sets into a portfolio view — giving leadership both the big picture and the ability to drill into any individual brand. Cross Brand Activation (Milestone 2) goes further by enabling cross-brand segmentation using the Global Profile, so you can identify customers who exist across multiple brands and build campaigns around that behavior without compromising brand-level reporting integrity.

4
Channel configuration can’t be separated per brand Every brand shares the same domains, phone numbers, and settings — with no way to configure them independently.
Click to expand+
❌ The Problem

Sending domains, click-tracking domains, SMS phone numbers, sender IDs, and third-party app integrations are all configured at the account level in Klaviyo. There is no sub-brand or brand-level configuration layer inside a single account. Every brand is locked into shared infrastructure — you cannot give Brand A its own sending domain while Brand B uses a different one, you cannot connect Brand A to a different Shopify store than Brand B, and you cannot assign separate SMS numbers per brand. This is a hard architectural limit. Customers managing 50+ brands like COTY face this as a daily operational burden — logging into every account one by one to update configurations that should be manageable in one place.

✅ How Organizations Solves It

Centralized Channel Settings Management (Milestone 2) allows org admins to configure sending domains, click-tracking domains, SMS phone numbers, and sender IDs at the organization level and push them to the relevant accounts. Instead of logging into 50 accounts to update a domain setting, you do it once and it propagates. Critically, each brand still has its own isolated configuration — they are not shared — but the management of those configurations is centralized at the org level. This directly eliminates the manual tax customers like COTY described. Each brand gets its own sending domain, its own channel settings, and its own integration — all manageable from a single org control panel.

5
Integrations require a 1:1 connection per brand Each ecommerce store needs its own dedicated Klaviyo account — one Shopify store maps to one Klaviyo account with no exceptions.
Click to expand+
❌ The Problem

Klaviyo’s integrations are built on a strict 1:1 architecture. Each ecommerce platform connection — Shopify, BigCommerce, Magento, and others — must be linked to a single dedicated Klaviyo account. There is no way to connect multiple Shopify stores to one Klaviyo account and maintain clean data separation, accurate order tracking, and reliable revenue sync. Forcing multiple brands and their respective stores into one account breaks the integration entirely — product catalogs merge, order data conflicts across brands, and revenue attribution becomes unreliable at the brand level. This is not a missing feature. It is a deliberate constraint that protects data integrity.

✅ How Organizations Solves It

Organizations preserves the 1:1 integration architecture by design — each Shopify store, BigCommerce site, or other ecommerce platform remains connected to its own dedicated Klaviyo account. This ensures product catalogs stay clean per brand, order data syncs accurately, and revenue attribution is reliable at the brand level. What Organizations adds on top is the umbrella layer — all those 1:1 connected accounts roll up into one Organization where leadership has unified visibility, centralized user management, and cross-brand analytics. Customers get the integration integrity they need AND the centralized control they want, without compromising either.

What Organizations Delivers — and When

Milestone 1 · End of Q2 2026 Committed
  • One place to manage all accounts, users, and permissions
  • Enterprise-grade permissions and access controls
  • Cross-brand billing visibility
  • Foundation for cross-brand reporting
Milestone 2 · Late 2026 / Early 2027 Planned
  • Send campaigns and flows across multiple brands at once
  • Unified customer profiles across all brands (Global Profile)
  • Centralized domain and channel settings management
  • Cross-brand segmentation and audience activation

Milestone 1 directly addresses the centralization and reduced friction customers are asking for today.

The Path Forward

1

Reframe the conversation

When customers ask for multiple sending domains in one account, the real ask is centralization and reduced operational friction. Help them see that one account per brand is the right architecture — and that Organizations solves the management overhead they’re actually complaining about.

2

Point to Milestone 1 — end of Q2 2026

Organizations M1 directly addresses the most common pain points: a single place to manage all accounts, enterprise-grade permissions, cross-brand billing visibility, and the data foundations needed for cross-brand reporting. This is committed and shipping end of Q2 2026.

3

Set expectations on Milestone 2 — late 2026 / early 2027

M2 delivers the deeper capabilities customers are asking for: cross-brand campaign deployment, unified customer profiles across brands, and centralized domain and channel settings management. This is planned for late 2026 / early 2027.

4

Offer Organizations as the solution today

For customers threatening churn or blocked on this issue, position Organizations as the strategic answer. Share the roadmap, get them on the M1 beta list, and anchor the conversation around what’s coming — not what’s missing today.

Organizations isn’t a workaround. It’s the right architecture — built to eliminate the silo tax and scale across every channel and product Klaviyo offers.
