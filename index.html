Why One Account Can’t Support Multiple Brands

Klaviyo’s account model is intentionally built around a single brand. Here’s what breaks when you force multiple brands into one.

1
Consent and suppression breaks across brands One unsubscribe silences a customer across every brand — even ones they never opted out of.
+

Klaviyo’s suppression logic operates at the account level. If a customer unsubscribes from Brand A, that suppression does not automatically transfer to Brand B in a separate account — which is the correct behavior. But inside a single account, one unsubscribe suppresses that profile globally, meaning a customer who opts out of Brand A emails is silently suppressed from Brand B, C, and D with no way to separate consent by brand. This creates compliance risk and kills cross-brand marketing effectiveness.

2
Sender reputation bleeds between brands One brand’s spam complaints damage deliverability for every other brand sharing the account.
+

Sending domain reputation is tied to the account. If Brand A runs a poorly targeted campaign that generates high spam complaints or bounces, it degrades the sender score for the shared domain — directly hurting the deliverability of Brand B and Brand C. There is no way to isolate one brand’s sending behavior from another inside a single account. Separate accounts with separate sending domains is the only way to protect each brand’s reputation independently.

3
Reporting and attribution becomes unreliable Revenue and engagement blend together — you can’t tell which brand is winning or losing.
+

All analytics in Klaviyo are scoped to a single account. Revenue attribution, flow performance, campaign engagement, and profile metrics would all be aggregated across every brand in the account with no clean way to separate them. Leadership loses the ability to evaluate brand-level performance, identify underperforming brands, or make data-driven investment decisions across the portfolio. Clean reporting requires clean data separation — which requires separate accounts.

4
Channel configuration can’t be separated per brand Every brand shares the same domains, phone numbers, and settings — with no way to configure them independently.
+

Sending domains, click-tracking domains, SMS phone numbers, sender IDs, and third-party app integrations are all configured at the account level. There is no sub-brand or brand-level configuration layer inside a single account. This means every brand is locked into shared infrastructure — you cannot give Brand A its own sending domain while Brand B uses a different one, and you cannot connect Brand A to a different Shopify store than Brand B. This is a hard architectural limit, not a missing feature.
